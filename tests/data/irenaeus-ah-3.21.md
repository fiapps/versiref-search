# Chapter XXI

**That Christ was not born of Joseph but of the Virgin, in accordance with the prophecy of Isaiah**

God, then, was made man, and the Lord did himself save us, giving us the token of the Virgin.
Not as some allege, among those now presuming to expound the Scripture, that it reads, *Behold, a young woman shall conceive, and bring forth a son* [Is 7:14], as Theodotion the Ephesian has interpreted, and Aquila of Pontus, both Jewish proselytes.
The Ebionites, following these, assert that he was begotten by Joseph, thus destroying, so far as in them lies, such a marvellous dispensation of God.

For the prophet did not merely say, *A virgin shall conceive*, but *a virgin shall conceive and bear a son* [Is 7:14].
It was not Joseph, then, but the Lord himself who gave a sign to the house of David; for he who was from David trembled through amazement at the message.[^1]
Moreover, the sign which God gave was not made through human intervention, for *that which is conceived in her is of the Holy Spirit* [Mt 1:20].

[^1]: Alexander Roberts and William Rambaut, trans., *Irenæus against Heresies* 3.21.4, in *Ante-Nicene Fathers*, vol. 1, ed. Alexander Roberts and James Donaldson (Buffalo: Christian Literature Publishing, 1885), 452.

Matthew, too, when speaking of the birth of Christ, says: *Now this took place to fulfil what the Lord had spoken by the prophet: Behold, the virgin shall conceive and bear a son, and they shall call his name Emmanuel* [Mt 1:22–23], thus confirming the promise made to Abraham.

> But this did confound all who deny the divine economy and disbelieve the prophets, that their prediction of the sign should have received so clear a fulfilment.[^2]

[^2]: Roberts and Rambaut, *Irenæus against Heresies* 3.21.4, 453.

And again Luke says, in announcing the message of the angel to the Virgin: *He will be great and will be called the Son of the Most High, and the Lord God will give to him the throne of his father David, and he will reign over the house of Jacob for ever, and of his kingdom there will be no end* [Lk 1:32–33].
For who else reigns uninterruptedly over the house of Jacob for ever but Christ Jesus our Lord, the Son of the Most High God, who promised through the law and the prophets that he would make his salvation visible to all flesh?[^3]

[^3]: Roberts and Rambaut, *Irenæus against Heresies* 3.21.5, 453.

If, then, the first Adam had a human father and was born of human seed, it were reasonable to say that the second Adam was begotten of Joseph.
But if the former was taken from the dust and fashioned by the Word of God, it was fitting that the Word himself, making a recapitulation in himself of Adam, should have a like mode of entry into existence.

Why, then, did God not again take dust, but wrought so that the formation should be made of Mary?
It was that there might not be another formation called into being nor any other which should require salvation, but that the very same formation should be summed up, the analogy having been preserved.[^4]

[^4]: Roberts and Rambaut, *Irenæus against Heresies* 3.21.10, 454.
